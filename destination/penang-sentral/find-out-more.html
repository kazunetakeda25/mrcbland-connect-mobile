My Journey of Discovery To Penang

I love travelling cross-country in trains. Zeke and I thought it’d be a great way to travel to Penang. We were advised to board one from KL Sentral, the city’s largest transportation hub.

It was here that we boarded the KTM’s ETS (Electric Train Service) which took just about 4 hours to reach Penang from KL Sentral. For the comfort and speed of the service, the tickets were reasonably priced at RM79 for the Platinum category and RM59 for the Gold category. A great deal.

We arrived at KL Sentral before our departure at 11.15 am. Since we had time to spare, we went browsing in a bookstore and stocked up snacks and water, there was even a mall next to KL Sentral called NU Sentral, where we tried the famed ‘bubble milk tea’ from one of the stores.

At 11 am we decided to walk back to the KTM station to wait for the train. Unlike catching a flight, we didn’t have to rush and worry about checking in baggage. The ETS interior was delightfully modern and clean, with overhead racks to put our bags. I noticed larger luggage can be stored near the entrance doors, so the aisle was free from obstruction.

At the front of the compartment is a LED screen, showing the latest movies, like in a plane. I like the compartments underneath our seats – that ensured our phones are fully charged and to power up laptops.

Being the only eatery in the train, the small café in Coach C draws an endless stream of customers. Not surprising since it serves a good selection of quick snacks like nasi lemak, tomato rice and fried noodles with drinks.

Zeke and I couldn’t stop taking snapshots of the lovely scenery along the way. The countryside is so green, and the landscape changes from time to time as the ETS passed through many quaint villages and towns. Sometimes the friendly folks especially kids even waved at us as the train whizzed past. Overall, it was a smooth ride, especially at high speed between stops at major train stations. From the on-screen information, I learned the ETS top speed is around 140kph. With a toilet in every second compartment, it’s as convenient as any airplane.

After the comfortable 4 hour 15 min journey, we reached Penang Sentral in Butterworth. We took a leisurely stroll across a foot-link bridge to the ferry terminal. The clear directional signages helped tremendously and we had no problems catching the iconic ferry to Penang island.

Penang Sentral is more than a transportation hub. It even has a shopping mall on-site with plenty of fast-food restaurants. So Zeke and I grabbed a quick bite before we purchased tickets for our ferry ride at the counter.

Boarding the ferry is as easy as walking in the park. Passengers can wheel their luggage when they embark and occupy whatever available seats on board. It’s fascinating to know cars and motorcycles can be driven right onto the ferries for the 20-minute crossing. On that clear sunny day, the sea breeze blowing in kept us cool. Our excitement grew as the ferry ride came to an end and we caught sight of Penang island. After leaving the Raja Tun Uda Ferry Terminal, we hopped into a Grabcar to get to our hotel in Lorong Selamat, Georgetown.

Feeling a tad warm after check-in, I suggested to Zeke we try cooling down with Ais Kacang, a local dessert of shaved ice drizzled with syrup, coconut milk, palm sugar and various goodies: red beans, diced jellies, sweet corn, etc. What a delicious way to counter the hot, humid weather! We were recommended to go to Sin Kim San Coffee Centre, a local coffee shop where we sampled Penang’s famous Prawn Mee and Char Kway Tiao.

Choices are aplenty there but both Zeke and I look forward to continue our food discovery journey!
