Brickfelds Story, Two Poles Becoming One

Brickfelds is one of the most colourful places in Kuala Lumpur, indicated by the variety of building types found there as well as the mix of its people. After all, Brickfelds has existed since 1881 when the Founder of KL, Kapitan Yap Ah Loy set up a brick-making factory to meet the new building requirements for KL after the great fre during that year.

It is notable that the iconic civic buildings at Dataran Merdeka, like the 1894 Sultan Abdul Samad building are built with bricks from Brickfelds.

Although Brickfelds has been largely represented by old buildings, Modernity came into the area like a ‘big bang’ with the development of KL Sentral, creating two opposite poles of activities and interests.

KL Sentral is Kuala Lumpur’s major transport interchange with a public transportation network that links Brickfelds to the rest of Peninsular Malaysia and through the KLIA Ekspres, to any part of the globe.

There is a juxtaposition of the new and old. KL Sentral is a metropolis of high- rise commercial buildings whereas the traditional areas are known for the unique mix of religious buildings, especially temples and churches, as well as traditional eateries. For example, all the modern F&B outlets can be found at KL Sentral, whereas, the old Kopitiam and a variety of Indian and Mamak restaurants are found within Brickfelds. Indeed, just a decade ago, when anyone in KL mention banana leaf rice or fsh head curry, thoughts immediately gravitate towards Brickfelds. The area is also famous for old Chinese vendor food like the soft pliable soya bean curd (tau foo far) along with standard Malay fare like nasi lemak.

All the Malaysian ethnic groups are represented in Brickfelds and among the most prominent are the Sri Lankans. From the community is Malaysia’s frst woman elected lawmaker, Tan Sri Devaki Krishnan who still lives in the distinguishable bungalow at Jalan Berhala.

A prominent character in the neighbouhood, she makes her rounds at the various community centres and temples and churches. Her neighbours are people from all walks of life, including the family of Tan Sri T. Ananda Krishnan, the sucessfull business tycoon of Maxis Communications.

As a long-time resident of Brickfelds, Tan Sri Devaki has witnessed at frst hand, the transformation of Brickfelds from a lazy outback in the 1950’s and 1960’s to a modern cosmopolitan powerhouse, which is also a major gateway to Kuala Lumpur.

She cited MRCB Land as the developer of KL Sentral and said that the development has had a positive efect on Brickfelds, spurring on the transformation of nearby slums into upmarket areas. Security has also improved with the uplifting of the local economy along with better maintenance of roads and drains. She is also a benefciary of the River of Life project that has now reached Brickfelds, and the river runs just behind her house.

The developments in Brickfelds have had a transformative efect on the surrounding areas, boosting property prices at Seputeh as well as Jalan Klang Lama, which also see the whole Jalan Klang Lama area including Taman Desa and Taman OUG being uplifted.
