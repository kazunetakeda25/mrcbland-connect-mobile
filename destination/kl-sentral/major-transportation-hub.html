KL Sentral a Major Transportation Hub

Today, KL Sentral situated in the Central Business District of Kuala Lumpur is Malaysia’s largest transportation hub, shining as the leading exemplar of the TOD model.

Transit-Oriented Developments or TOD simply put, is an urban development that maximises the amount of residential, business and leisure areas within walking distance of public transport. In a world, where 55% of the world’s population are living in cities, increasing to 68%; and where Malaysia’s urbanisation rate is to reach 80% by 2020, TODs are becoming increasingly necessary.

Perhaps the greatest thing that a TOD achieves is prioritising people over cars. By having all the necessary amenities within 5-10 minutes walking distance of an MRT station for example, drastically reduces the need for cars. By that token, a TOD unleashes a series of domino efects of benefts to the communities at large.

All at once, the environment improves with less carbon footprint and more space for living. Higher density at TOD areas would free up land elsewhere for recreation and parks. Air quality improves around the TODs while work and leisure become more concentrated in smaller areas, enabling a more holistic and cosmopolitan lifestyle.

By further increasing integration by building allocated pedestrian walkways from train stations to bus networks, a TOD is more freely enjoyed when shopping complexes, hotels, residences, ofices as well as cafes and restaurants are within a stone’s throw away.

KL Sentral’s skyline is always framed from afar by the twin hotels - Hilton KL and Le Meridien. It doesn’t stop here as the smorgasbord of hotels also include Ascott Sentral KL, The St. Regis and Aloft KL Sentral.

Additionally, KL Sentral has attracted a plethora of businesses to set up their ofices in this location. From startup ofices to banks and fnancial institutions. Large local corporations also shape the silhouette of KL Sentral as UEM Group, Malakof, Tenaga Nasional and Khanazah Nasional have set up their headquarters there. It has also become the convergence of multiple co-working spaces - Common Ground, Colony, Regus, Unispace, CEO Suite and the recently launched Found8.

The new wine has not caused the old wineskin to burst as the synergy has resulted in exponential benefts for both the old and new Brickfelds, situated near KL Sentral. KL Sentral’s TOD universe has brought economic and infrastructure benefts to old Brickfelds while the old Brickfelds provide the quaint and the charm to make living more enjoyable.

Since the advent of KL Sentral, it became the leading exponent for TOD success such that the model has been adopted for urban developments in Malaysia, even by other developers. KL Sentral defnes what the ‘Malaysian TOD’ looks like, with the network of life at the ground and lower levels, and towers of habitat and workplaces above. It forges new metropolitan communities composed of people that call the TOD, “my home”.
