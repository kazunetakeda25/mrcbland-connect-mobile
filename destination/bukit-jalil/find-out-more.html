SPORTS IN A Transport Oriented Development (TOD)

Largest sports complex in the country—KL Sports City—the sprawling complex houses five stadiums, three different parks, and the Bukit Jalil Sports School.

SPORTS IN A TOD

Sometimes life can get hectic, and we forget to take care of ourselves. It is easy to get lost in work stress and personal challenges tend to take over. Growing up as an active kid and playing badminton competitively both locally and internationally, I have always believed that one of the best ways to keep our minds and bodies healthy is through an active lifestyle. After all, a healthy body often equates to a healthy brain.

Living in Bukit Jalil inspires me to maintain my active lifestyle. Home to the largest sports complex in the country—KL Sports City—the sprawling complex houses five stadiums, three different parks, and the Bukit Jalil Sports School. With so many world-class stadiums and sports complexes, it is no wonder so many international events from music concerts to international sporting competitions are regularly held here. Besides, the numerous parks, bicycle paths, and multiple running tracks makes it a good spot for a morning run or an evening bike ride.

Running Track

Early bird catches the worm! I wake up as early as 6:30 in the morning for a run. I usually put on some fast-paced music in my earphones to get my blood pumping and my mind ready. Sometimes, I recall my university days, when I would wake up in the early hours of the morning to train with my teammates on the university track. It’s always tough to commit to exercise, but the sense of achievement of a good workout is hard to beat. “Sore today, stronger tomorrow”, right?

By 7am, I arrive at the running track at the Bukit Jalil Stadium. The first five minutes is always the hardest, but as I shake off the lethargy and as the serotonin kicks in, I reach a mental state that transcends my worries and anxieties. Some call it “runner’s high”, but what’s important is creating habits and being disciplined with fitness.

Iconic Bukit Jalil

By 8.30am, I’m all showered and ready to head to work. This is when I treat myself for after an early morning workout and nourish my body with the nutrients it needs. I’ll have some whey protein, oats, peanut butter and a banana, all blended into a nutritious smoothie—a perfect way to start the day. What’s even better is that living close to a TOD like Bukit Jalil with excellent connectivity and multiple public transport options including the LRT, getting to work is a breeze.

Strength Training

Of course, an active lifestyle is not complete with running alone. Strength training is an essential part of any healthy fitness routine. So two to three times a week after work, I head to the gym. What’s great is that there are plenty of gyms in Bukit Jalil, from established international fitness chains to smaller pay-per-entry gyms—all easily accessible by car or public transport.

The body and mind are interconnected systems. The body is the vehicle to which we move about in the world, and strengthening our body serves to increase the intensity and speed of our actions and thinking. A strong body augments the mind while a weak body is a like deadweight to the mind.

Connect with Nature

Perhaps one of life’s simple joys can be found in connection with nature. During the weekends, I take advantage of the numerous nearby hiking trails, such as Bukit Jalil Recreational Park or the Bukit Serdang hiking trail, both within minutes of Bukit Jalil. Hiking and exploring these trails allows me a chance to get in a workout while enjoying the great outdoors and connecting with nature—a privilege that many Malaysians often lack.

Wholesome Eating

When it comes time to chow down, Bukit Jalil is filled with a number of great eateries serving a diverse range of cuisines. From Taiwanese food at the RedDrip Bakery cafe to authentic Nyonya favourites at Baba Wee, it’s not hard to find your new favourite spot in Bukit Jalil’s vibrant food scene.

One of my personal go-to spots for the occasional cheat meal is local gourmet burger joint Define:Burgers. My usual order? The signature cheeseburger, with an additional beef patty for an extra protein boost.

Bukit Jalil TOD

Bukit Jalil is an amazing place to live in. It is truly a place where all athletes come to be inspired. Built in 1997, ahead of the 1998 Commonwealth Games, Bukit Jalil Stadium is the largest stadium in Southeast Asia with a capacity of 100,000. After its rejuvenation in 2017 by Populous and MRCB, the sports complex, now a part of KL Sports City, was named ‘Stadium of The Year’ at the World Stadium Congress Awards in Amsterdam. KL Sports City also comprises of the National Aquatic Centre, Axiata Arena, National Hockey stadium and more. These iconic buildings are home to world-class international events where people from all walks of life come to take part in the electrifying atmosphere.

As a little boy, it was here where I watched the Malaysian Open badminton event in 2004 that I decided to make sports a permanent fixture of my life.
